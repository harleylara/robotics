## Elimination

To be able to to forward elimination we need a pivot, to begin the pivot is $\mA_{1,1}$. We need to find a way to make $\mA_{2,1}$ zero, and this can be done using a multiplier and substract the result.

In this example the multiplier is the number $3$.

<Equation
    formula="\begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    0 & 2 & -2 \\
    0 & 4 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Forward elimination step 1."
/>

For the pivot $\mA_{2,2}$ we use the multiplier $2$.

<Equation
    formula="\mU = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    0 & 2 & -2 \\
    0 & 0 & 5
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Forward elimination step 2."
/>

The resulting matrix is usually refers as \mU$ for upper triangular.

<Important
    note="Pivots **CAN NOT** be zero."
/>

WAIT a second, these are equations what happen with the right hand side (rhs)?... We also need to apply the same operations in the same order to the vector $\vb$ (rhs).

<Equation
    formula="\vb = \begin{bmatrix}
    2 \\
    12 \\
    2
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Right hand side of the system of linear equations."
/>

After applying the same operations to $\vb$, the following vector is obtained:

<Equation
    formula="\vc = \begin{bmatrix}
    2 \\
    6 \\
    -10
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Right hand side of the system of linear equations after applying same operations in $\mA$."
/>

## Back-Substitution

So now with $\mU$ and $\vc$ let's rewrite the system of linear equations:

<Equation
    formula="\begin{cases}
    \begin{aligned}
    x + 2y + z &= 2 \\
    2y -2z &= 6 \\
    5z &= -10
    \end{aligned}
    \end{cases}"
    description="System of linear equations after elimination."
/>

How do we solve this for $x, y, z$ ?... simple right ?

<Equation
    formula="\begin{aligned}
    x &= 2 \\
    y &= 1 \\
    z &= -2
    \end{aligned}"
    description="Solution for our system of linear equations."
/>

This process id called **back-substitution**.

## Elimination Matrices

Wouldn't it be cool if we could put the manual operations we did earlier into a matrix ?

<Equation
    formula="\begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 0 \\
    -3 & 1 & 0 \\
    0 & 0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    3 & 8 & 1 \\
    0 & 4 & 1
    \end{bmatrix} = 
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    0 & 2 & -2 \\
    0 & 4 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Step 1 of the forward elimination using elimination matrices."
/>

Let's call this elimination/elementary matrix $\mE_{2,1}$.
<Equation
    formula="\mE_{2,1} = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 0 \\
    -3 & 1 & 0 \\
    0 & 0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Elimination matrix $\mE_{2,1}$"
/>

<Equation
    formula="\begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 0 \\
    0 & 1 & 0 \\
    0 & -2 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    0 & 2 & -2 \\
    0 & 4 & 1
    \end{bmatrix} = 
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 1 \\
    0 & 2 & -2 \\
    0 & 0 & 5
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Step 2 of the forward elimination."
/>

<Equation
    formula="\mE_{3,2} = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 0 \\
    0 & 1 & 0 \\
    0 & -2 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Elimination matrix $\mE_{3,2}$"
/>

Means that we can write the operations as follows:

<Equation
    formula="\mE_{3,2} (\mE_{2,1} \mA) = \mU"
    description="Using matrix operations for matrices elimination."
/>

We can change the paranthesis:

<Equation
    formula="(\mE_{3,2} \mE_{2,1}) \mA = \mU"
    description="By the associative law of matrices."
/>


<Important
    note="You **CANT NOT** change the order."
/>

## Permutation Matrices

If we have the matrix:

<Equation
    formula="\begin{bmatrix}
    a & b \\
    c & d
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Example matrix for permutation."
/>

What matrix can we use to exchange **row 1** with **row 2**?

<Equation
    formula="\begin{bmatrix}
    0 & 1 \\
    1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    a & b \\
    c & d
    \end{bmatrix} = 
    \begin{bmatrix}
    c & d \\
    a & b
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Example of permutation operation."
/>

Suppose we want to do columns instead... How can we do that?

<Equation
    formula="
    \begin{bmatrix}
    a & b \\
    c & d
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    0 & 1 \\
    1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix} =
    \begin{bmatrix}
    b & a \\
    d & c
    \end{bmatrix}"
    description="Example of permutation operation."
/>

Can you notice a pattern ?... Yep.
- For **row** operations the matrix goes to the **left**. 
- For **column** operations the matrix goes to the **right**
