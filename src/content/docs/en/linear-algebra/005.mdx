## Transpose

<Equation>
    $$
    \textrm{If} \quad \mA = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 3 \\
    0 & 0 & 4
    \end{bmatrix} \quad \textrm{then} \quad \mA^{T} = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 \\
    2 & 0 \\
    3 & 4
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$
    Transpose.
</Equation>

General formula:

<Equation>
    $$
    (\mA^T)_{ij} = \mA_{ji}
    $$
    General formula for transpose.
</Equation>

<Equation>
    $$
    \mA^T = \mA
    $$
    Symetric matrix.
</Equation>

<Important>
    Transpose do not effect symetric matrices.
</Important>

A matrix multiplied by the transpose produce a symetric matrix.

<Equation>
    $$
    \mR^{T} \mR \textrm{ is always symetric}
    $$
</Equation>

Rule to transpose:

<Equation>
    $$
    (\mR^{T} \mR)^{T} = \mR^{T} \mR^{TT} = \mR^{T} \mR
    $$
</Equation>

## Vector Spaces

space: refers to a set (space) of vectors.

Vector space requirements, the sum of any vector $\vv + \vw$ and the multiplication $c\vv$ **stay in the space**.

In other words: All linear combinations of $c\vv + d\vw$ are **in the space**.


Example:
- $\sR^{2}$: all posible 2-dim **real** column vectors. Represent a plane.
- $\sR^{n}$: all posible n-dim **real** column vectors.

<Important>
    Definition: The space $\sR^n$ consists of all column vectors $\vv$ with $n$ components (the components are real numbers).
</Important>

<VectorSpace2D>
    $\sR^{2}$ vector space.
</VectorSpace2D>

Subspaces of $\sR^{2}$
- all of $\sR^{2}$
- any line through $[0, 0]$
- the zero vector

<SubSpaceL2D>
    Any LINE through $[0,0]$ is a sub-space of the vector space $\sR^{2}$.
</SubSpaceL2D>
