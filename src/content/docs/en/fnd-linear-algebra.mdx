## Vector

> In mathematics, physics, and engineering, a Euclidean vector or simply a vector (sometimes called a geometric vector or spatial vector) is a geometric object that has magnitude (or length) and direction.
source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_vector

Operations on vectors:
- Scalar multiplication
- Addition and Subtraction
- Length
- Normalized vector
- Dot product
- Cross product

### Dot Product

<Equation
    formula="\va \cdot \vb = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_{i} b_{i}"
    description="Coordinate definition of the dot product."
/>

<Equation
    formula="\va \cdot \vb = \Vert \va \Vert \cdot \Vert \vb \Vert cos\theta"
    description="Geometric definition of the dot product. Where $\Vert \va \Vert$ and $\Vert \vb \Vert$ are the euclidean norm and $\theta$ is the angle between the vectors"
/>

$$
\vx \cdot \vy = \begin{Vmatrix} \vx \end{Vmatrix} \begin{Vmatrix} \vy \end{Vmatrix} \cos{\theta}
$$

if $\vx \cdot \vy = 0$, $\vx$ and $\vy$ are orthogonal

$$
\cos{\theta} = \frac{\vx \cdot \vy}{\begin{Vmatrix} \vx \end{Vmatrix} \begin{Vmatrix} \vy \end{Vmatrix}}
$$
